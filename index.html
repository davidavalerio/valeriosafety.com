Hazard Elimination

We prioritize high-energy hazards—the ones that actually kill people. Struck-by, caught-between, falls, energy release. Not the low-risk items that generate paperwork but change nothing.

Safety Programs That Work

JSAs, hazard assessments, and emergency response plans designed for your operations—not copied from a template. Programs crews will actually use because they address real risks they recognize.

Training With Substance

Incident Investigation

David Valerio

I'm an HSE professional based in Williston, North Dakota, working across oil and gas operations in the Williston Basin. I started Valerio Safety because I got tired of watching safety programs that exist to satisfy auditors rather than protect workers.

Too much of what passes for safety is theater. Regulations designed to protect regulators. Metrics that look rigorous but predict nothing. I take a different approach: focus resources on eliminating high-energy hazards—the things that actually cause fatalities and serious injuries. Meet compliance obligations efficiently, but don't confuse passing an audit with keeping people safe.

This isn't anti-safety. It's anti-theater. The goal is fewer people hurt, not more paperwork generated.
